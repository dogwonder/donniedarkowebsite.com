OBITUARIES

DARKO, DONALD J. (1972-1988)

Died in his sleep in the early morning hours of October 2, at his family's home on Powderham Drive, in the Sarasota Heights subdivision of Middlesex, when the engine of an aircraft detached and fell through the roof of the residence. He was sixteen.

Investigators from the Federal Aviation Administration have so far been unable to match the engine to any registered aircraft. No carrier has reported a loss. The FAA has declined further comment, citing an open investigation.

A junior at Middlesex Ridge School, Donald is remembered by classmates as quiet and sharp-witted, and by his teachers as a student given to questions they could not always answer.

He is survived by his parents, Edward and Rose Darko, and his sisters Elizabeth and Samantha.

A neighbor, Roberta Sparrow, 101, a retired Middlesex Ridge science teacher, when reached for comment, said only: "Every living creature on earth dies alone."

FAA TEAM EXTENDS STAY IN MIDDLESEX

The federal team investigating the October 2 incident on Powderham Drive has extended its stay in Middlesex indefinitely, a spokesman confirmed Wednesday. Residents report the crash site remains cordoned. The origin of the engine is still unknown.
