level 4 — “cellar door” · design notes

← the spread

An epilogue level. Six fixed scenes on the 800×500 stage, played in order. The player is Donnie in the restored timeline — everything undone, but he remembers. Grief disguised as a puzzle.

Three moods carry the level: the void (flat black), the document (paper gradient), the desktop (Win98 windows on black). One red — --highlight #fc0d1b — per screen, maximum. Red means clickable. It is the only signpost the level offers.

01

the collapse

the void
/cellar/door/
compose
Flat black --dark. A single text block at left 12% / top 33%, width 54%. Ceremonial date in grey #565656 (12px, tracking 0.42em); three lines of assembling type in dread-grey #7c7c7c (14px), the last clause lifted to #cccccc. Credit pinned top-right; 1px scanlines over all.
copy
10 . 02 . 1988 the tangent universe collapsed. everything you did is undone. and yet — you remember. .continue.
reveal
0.7s hold on black, then words assemble one at a time at a 150ms cadence. On the final word, .continue. fades in over 0.6s.
the red
.continue. — the only way out of the void, and the only red on screen.
02

the letter

the document · paper
/cellar/door/letter.html
compose
Paper gradient --paper (#ddddcc→#fff). A typewritten letter in --mono-alt (DepartureMono), ink #1d1a16, 13px, at left 21% / top 11%, width 58%. Date and signature right-aligned in #4a463e. Faint scanlines give the scanned-document feel.
copy
10 . 02 . 1988 donnie — the world did not end. you unmade it. you carried the dark back through itself and the count began again from nothing. i wrote that every living creature on earth dies alone. that was not the whole of it. the dead wait behind a door you have opened before. you will know the words when you stand there — the most beautiful in the language. say them at the cellar door. — r. sparrow
reveal
Lines type in one at a time (360ms) with a blinking ink caret tracking the active line; a click completes the page instantly. After the last line, the key phrase recolors to red over 0.5s and becomes the door forward.
the red
“cellar door” — the hint is the secret (the words for scene 04), and the red word is also the link onward. Sparrow’s register never names the mechanic.
03

the hunt

the void
/cellar/door/sparrow.html
compose
Near-total darkness. One faint line dead-centre in #262626. A single red mark — the crosshair, 16px, --highlight — at 32% opacity, placed randomly (12–82% x, 16–78% y) on every visit. Timestamp burned bottom-left; credit dimmed to #3a3a3a.
copy
her name was that of a bird.
reveal
Nothing moves. The mark brightens to full and flickers on hover. Clicking it → the whisper. The loneliest screen in the level — you have to find the door in the dark.
the red
the one faint mark. It is moved every visit, so the hunt is never the same place twice.
04

the whisper

the desktop · win98
/cellar/door/whisper.html
compose
A single 98.css window — title whisper, navy title bar, close box — centred on the void --dark. Body: a prompt, a text field, an ok button. Nothing else exists.
copy
whisper ✕ say the words. [ ] ok › those are not the words. › try to remember. › she told you once.
reveal
The field is auto-focused; Enter or ok submits. The answer is cellar door (case- and space-insensitive). Wrong answers throw a native Win98 alert — the system is the fiction. The close box refuses: “YOU CANNOT CLOSE THIS.”
the red
none — deliberately withheld. The only screen with no signpost. The level has taught you the words; here you must already know them.
05

frank

the desktop
/cellar/door/frank.html
compose
One lone frank desktop icon top-left. A window titled FRANK centred, with a red is-warn title bar (#7a0000→--highlight) and a black inset screen. Inside: Frank’s uppercase voice and a large count in --mono-alt, all in --highlight.
copy
wake up, donnie. i have been watching. 28 : 06 : 42 : 12 → 00 : 00 : 00 : 00 that is when the world ends. you are out of time. .open the door.
reveal
Frank’s voice assembles (130ms), the count fades in and drains to zero over ~5.2s on an accelerating curve — time collapsing inward. At zero the count glitches (SVG turbulence) as the intro voice fades out and the verdict lands; then .open the door. fades in. Close box → “YOU MADE ME DO IT.”
the red
Frank himself. His voice and the draining count are the screen’s single red presence — the count is the end of the 28-day count, run all the way out.
06

the door

the void · terminal
/cellar/door/end.html
compose
A centred doorway of light built from hard vector bands — dark jambs #070707, grey step edges #3f3f3f/#b4b4b4, a pure-white 44px core, a bright lintel — with light spilling onto the floor in two hard-edged trapezoids (#c8c8c8, #7a7a7a). Held words bottom-left, embossed white #cdcdcd. No scanlines, no chrome, no animation.
copy
you said the words. the door is open.
reveal
None. It holds, perfectly still — the last frame of the site. There is no exit.
the red
spent. No red, and nowhere left to click. The signpost that guided the whole level is gone because the journey is over.

the arc

collapse › letter › darkness › whisper › countdown › light

constraints honoured

  • 800×500 stage; everything outside is void; all in-scene placement absolute, in % of stage.
  • One red --highlight per screen, maximum — and twice, deliberately, none.
  • Palette, type (Pixelated MS Sans Serif / DepartureMono), and components (98.css windows, hidden marks, assembling type, scanlines, turbulence) per the system.
  • Copy lowercase, terse, oracular, second person, present tense; dates as ritual; system voice UPPERCASE; the mechanic never named.
  • No gradients except paper + Win98 title bars; no rounded corners; no soft shadows; terminal state holds perfectly still.
